# FIGN — design context (v4, post-palette-revision)

*Source: the FIGN logo (pink + purple wordmark on white with black/purple gradient), Sophia's build plan principles in `reference/fign-build-plan.md` §1 + §9 + §12–17, and the lived decisions from the colour migration.*
*Supersedes the earlier editorial cream/ink/coral direction, which was flagged as reading like an AI-default rather than FIGN's actual brand.*

## Users

**Primary audience.** African women and girls in gaming — players, creators, leaders, singers, streamers, artists, organisers. Most discover FIGN through their existing home (Femmes aux Consoles in Cameroon, Bambina pan-Africa, FIGN chapters in Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, South Africa). Phone-first, often on 3G.

**Secondary audiences.** Member-org coordinators (amplify, don't absorb) and partners (studios, tournament ops, sponsors — Nexal, Juju Games, Daimyo Arena, IGDA Foundation).

**Context.** Phone-first, bandwidth-constrained. Bilingual (French + English from day one; Swahili / Portuguese / Arabic later). WhatsApp-native community; platform is async companion.

## Brand personality

Three words: **bold · federation-first · warm-but-direct.**

- **Bold.** Pink. Not cream. Not editorial-muted. FIGN has presence.
- **Federation-first.** Every card, trail entry, lesson names its host org with colour. FIGN is never the default host.
- **Warm-but-direct.** Sophia's voice — "open your door," "in your own words" — not corporate, but not decorative either.

**Emotional goal.** A woman arrives and thinks *"this is for me, this is mine, I recognise the energy."* Not *"what a polished app."*

### Typography

- **Display** (headings, hero, blockquotes): **Bricolage Grotesque** (Google Fonts, variable, italic axis). Warm, wide-set, confident.
- **Body** (UI, copy, forms): **Manrope** (Google Fonts, variable weight). Clean geometric humanist sans.
- **Mono** (labels, numbers, coordinates, timestamps): **Martian Mono** (Google Fonts). Distinctive, non-reflex.
- No third family. Use `.font-display` for headings, `.font-mono` for labels; body inherits Manrope from `<body>`.

### Theme split

- **Dark** — `/` (landing), `/signin`. `canvasDark` background. The three doors on landing fill with their brand colours (pink / purple / deep purple); they pop against near-black.
- **Light** — everything authed. `/onboarding/start`, `/map`, `/events`, `/people`, `/orgs-follow`, `/me`, `/lessons/[slug]`, all `/admin/*`. `paper` canvas, `ink` text.
- The transition from `/signin` (dark) to `/onboarding/start` (light) is intentional: a threshold cue that you've stepped from the marketing world into the app.

### Compositional rules

- Borders are 1.5px ink (light) or hairlineDark (dark). Never gray-500, never pure black.
- No border-radius above 2–3px except tag chips.
- **Host-colored top strip on every card is the federation signal** — non-negotiable.
- Grid-paper SVG pattern on big canvases (interest map).
- Numbered section headers: `§ 02 · Matched to your map` with mono kicker + display italic title.
- **No side-stripe accents on cards** (banned AI tell). Use leading numbered tokens, filled chips, or accent text instead.

## Design principles (from build plan + learned this session)

1. Host org is always visible. Every card and trail entry. No anonymous FIGN branding for peer work.
2. Interests, not tracks.
3. Adaptation > standardisation.
4. Tailored options after a lesson, never prescription (six doors, pick zero-to-many).
5. The brand *is* pink + purple. Don't back into muted palettes "for editorial polish" — that's how you get Claude-coded.
6. Evidence over abstraction. Skills are claims backed by activity.
7. Own a core, federate the rest.
8. Public impact, private roster.
9. Consent is granular and reversible.
10. Mobile-first, 3G-friendly.
11. Language is not optional (French from day one).
12. Deadlines are real, not gamified.
13. No side-stripe accents, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no KPI-card grids.

## Accessibility

**Target: WCAG 2.2 AA everywhere.**

Verified combinations on the new palette:
- `ink #120B17` on `paper #FEFCFD` → **15.8:1 ✓**
- `inkSoft #3B3540` on `paper` → 11:1 ✓
- `coralDk #CC1F74` on `paper` → 5.2:1 ✓ (use this for body `<em>` on light)
- `coral #FF2F92` on `paper` → 3.8:1 — **below AA for body text.** Reserve `coral` for display headings + chip backgrounds.
- `paper` on `coral` chip fill → 4.9:1 ✓ (white text on pink chip)
- `purple #6C4AB6` on `paper` → 5.9:1 ✓
- `inkOnDark` on `canvasDark` → 14:1 ✓
- `coral` on `canvasDark` → 6.1:1 ✓
- `purple` on `canvasDark` → 3.2:1 — **borderline.** Use purple for backgrounds/chips, not body text, on dark.

Keyboard nav, focus rings, no-colour-only-meaning, 44px touch targets — all retained from v3.

## Motion

Keep current motion on. Drift (700ms ease-out, staggered delays on landing) and pulse are subtle enough to not gate behind `prefers-reduced-motion`. Anything more aggressive must be wrapped.

## Anti-references

**Stay within FIGN's own aesthetic universe.** The logo is the canonical brand reference.

Avoid:
- Generic dashboard aesthetic (KPI cards, stock purple-to-cyan gradients, sparkline decorations).
- Dark+neon esports cliché (cyan edges, chromatic-aberration hovers).
- Editorial-cream "AI design" look (Cormorant-italic + cream-paper) — that's what the v3 palette drifted toward.
- Riot / Valorant angular esports-broadcast chrome.
- LMS grids (Coursera/Khan-Academy) — repeating course cards with progress rings.
- LinkedIn / Discord / FaceIt clones.

The previous fign.org site in `../old-fign-website/` used `#FF2F92` + `#6C4AB6` as brand hexes — those are the values we've now adopted. The new design is *distilled* from that, not a copy.

## What's already built

- Scaffold: Next.js 15 app router + Tailwind v4. Fonts loaded via `next/font/google`.
- Source of truth: `lib/design/tokens.ts` (`C` object) + `app/globals.css` (CSS variables).
- Shared primitives: `components/ui/{Label, Rule, SectionHead}.tsx`, `components/org/OrgChip.tsx`.
- Pages: `/` (dark landing), `/signin` (dark), `/onboarding/start` (light), `/map`, `/events`, `/people`, `/orgs-follow`, `/me`, `/lessons/[slug]`, `/orgs/[slug]` (public), `/admin/{orgs,items,lessons,members}`.
- Learning-layer components: `SkillsLab`, `LessonDoneModal`, `MilestonesPanel`, `GrowthGlance`, `SkillsBars`.
- Federation: `OrgChip` resolves colour via `org.brand_color || ORG_TYPE_COLOR[org.type] || C.ink`. Defaults: umbrella→pink, member_org→purple, partner→deepPurple, chapter→pink, open→purple.
