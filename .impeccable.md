# FIGN — design context

*Source: derived from Sophia Nei's `reference/fign-build-plan.md` §1, §9, §12–17, the three visual prototypes in `reference/`, and the palette already live in `lib/design/tokens.ts`. Update this file whenever the brief changes.*

## Users

**Primary audience.** African women and girls in gaming — players, creators, leaders, singers, streamers, artists, organisers. Many discover FIGN from their existing home (Femmes aux Consoles in Cameroon, Bambina pan-Africa, FIGN chapters in Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, South Africa). Most open the platform on a phone, often on patchy connections.

**Secondary audiences.** Member-organisation coordinators (who need to amplify their org without feeling assimilated) and partners (studios, tournament operators, sponsors — Nexal, Juju Games, Daimyo Arena, IGDA Foundation — who need a reach channel).

**Context of use.**
- Phone-first, bandwidth-constrained. 3G-friendly paint required.
- Bilingual reality: French from day one (Cameroon) alongside English; Swahili / Portuguese / Arabic later.
- WhatsApp-native community; the platform is async-companion to WhatsApp, not a replacement.
- A 16-year-old in Douala, a caster in Jo'burg, a chapter lead in Accra are all plausible "first opens."

## Brand personality

Three words: **editorial · warm · federation-first.**

- **Editorial**, not product-design-system. The app looks like a thoughtfully-set field journal, not a SaaS dashboard. Numbers and dates feel typeset, not telemetric.
- **Warm.** Coral for warmth, green for agency. Copy in Sophia's voice — "open your door," "in your own words," "a federation, not a funnel" — not corporate.
- **Federation-first.** Every card, trail entry, and lesson names its host org. FIGN is never the default host. Attribution is a design signal, not a footnote.

**Emotional goal.** A woman arrives and thinks: *"this was made for me, with care, by people who do the work."* Not: *"another platform for me to fill out a profile."*

## Aesthetic direction

**Visual language: editorial / field-journal.**

- **Cream paper `#f5ecdc`** as background. Never white.
- **Dark ink `#1a1410`** for text and borders. Never pure black.
- **Coral `#c94a2a`** — FIGN brand, warmth, emphasis (`<em>` is always coral).
- **Green `#1f7a3f`** — "here," agency, confirmation.
- **Purple `#6b3a7a`** — member orgs (FAC, Bambina).
- **Blue `#2b5a8a`** — partners (Nexal, Juju, Daimyo, Ingage, IGDA, etc.).
- **Gold `#a67c1e`** — open-source / CC-licensed content.
- **Ink-soft `#4a3d30`** body, **ink-mute `#8a7a68`** captions, **paper-alt `#ece1cc`** card surfaces, **paper-dk `#ddcfb4`** insets.

**Typography.**
- **Cormorant Garamond italic** for headings, blockquotes, emphatic display. Italic by default on headings.
- **JetBrains Mono** for labels, coordinates, numbers, timestamps, org brand tags. All-caps with `tracking: 0.18–0.22em` for labels.
- No third family. No icon-heavy UI.

**Compositional cues.**
- Borders are **1.5px ink**. Never `gray-500`, never pure black.
- No border-radius above 2–3px except tag chips.
- Host-colored top strip on every card is the federation signal — non-negotiable.
- Grid-paper SVG background pattern on big canvases (the interest map, the path view).
- Numbered section headers (`§ 02 · Matched to your map`) with mono kicker + serif italic title.

**References Sophia explicitly rejects (§1 of build plan):**
- *Walled membership club* aesthetics.
- *Generic "women-in-gaming" purple-gradient* look — FIGN refuses it.
- *KPI-card dashboards* with four metric tiles.
- *LinkedIn / Discord / FaceIt clones.*
- *AI-slop*: neon on dark, glass-morphism everywhere, emoji-first labels.

## Design principles (from build plan §9 + §16, pinned)

1. **Host org is always visible.** Every card, every link, every notification names the host org with its colored chip. No anonymous FIGN-branded content for work a peer did.
2. **Interests, not tracks.** Members pick from seven interest groups (Play, Create, Voice, Stream, Words, Look, Lead) or write their own words. Never forced into career tracks.
3. **Adaptation > standardisation.** The platform bends to the org's workflow. Registration can be the org's own system, a webhook, or FIGN-hosted — host's choice.
4. **Tailored options, no prescription.** When a lesson ends, offer six doors (make / reflect / go further / take it live / bring someone / bookmark). She picks zero, one, or many. Never push a next lesson.
5. **Editorial aesthetic, everywhere.** Cream paper, dark ink, serif italics, mono coordinates. Coral for warmth, green for agency. No neon, no gradients, no purple-gradient cliché.
6. **Evidence over abstraction.** Skills are claims backed by real activity — lessons completed, events attended, peer endorsements. No badges without proof, no levels without receipts.
7. **Own a core, federate the rest.** FIGN publishes only what is distinctively FIGN (welcome lessons, digital-safety content, community hosting guides). Everything else is attributed to its creator.
8. **Public impact, private roster.** `/impact` is public. Member rosters and private mentor notes are not.
9. **Consent is granular and reversible.** Visibility in rosters, public skill graphs, activity-trail visibility — all opt-in.
10. **Mobile-first, 3G-friendly.** Every page must paint meaningfully under bandwidth constraints. SSR the expensive bits; defer client hydration below the fold.
11. **Language is not optional.** French from day one alongside English.
12. **Deadlines are real, not gamified.** "In 4 days" because the opportunity closes, not because a streak breaks.

## Theme

**Light by default.** Cream paper. This is deliberate, not a safe default:
- Patchy bandwidth favors high-contrast, low-animation light UI.
- Editorial / field-journal aesthetic reads natively on paper.
- African women-in-gaming market is already saturated with dark + neon — light cream is the differentiator.

No dark-mode planned. If it happens, it's as a genuine re-design (e.g. an after-dark stream-companion surface), not a token flip.

## Accessibility

**Target: WCAG 2.2 AA minimum.** Every screen must meet:
- 4.5:1 contrast ratio on body text, 3:1 on non-text elements.
- Full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings.
- No colour-only meaning (host chips carry both colour and text label).
- Logical heading order, form labels wired to inputs.

The editorial palette already passes AA on cream paper: ink `#1a1410` on paper `#f5ecdc` is 15.8:1; coral `#c94a2a` on paper is 4.9:1; green `#1f7a3f` on paper is 5.2:1. Be careful when placing smaller text on `paper-alt #ece1cc` — verify contrast for each new component.

## Motion

**Keep current motion on.** The drift animation on the landing hero (700ms ease-out with staggered delays) and the pulse on "you are here" markers are subtle enough that Sophia chose not to wrap them in `prefers-reduced-motion`. Future animations should be held to the same bar: if it's gentler than a scroll-triggered parallax, it stays on. Anything more aggressive must be wrapped in a reduced-motion media query.

## Anti-references

Sophia's direction: **stay unique, but within FIGN's own aesthetic universe** — the one defined in `reference/fign-build-plan.md` §1 and the three prototypes. No external style references.

Things to actively preserve from that universe:
- The three-door landing pattern (`fign-landing.jsx`) as the canonical entry composition.
- The radial interest-map SVG from `fign-member-home.jsx` (declared / derived / adjacent rings).
- The federation card: host-colored top strip, co-hosts + endorsers as smaller chips, "Why you / Why this" reason line.
- Numbered section headers with mono kicker + serif italic title (`§ 02 · Matched to your map`).
- The founder-quote block composition (mono kicker → serif italic blockquote → coral attribution).

Things FIGN will never look like (from build plan §1):
- A walled membership club (FIGN is connective tissue, not a destination).
- A rigid career-track system.
- A Discord / LinkedIn / itch.io / FaceIt clone.
- A KPI-card dashboard.
- A generic purple-gradient "women in gaming" aesthetic — that market is crowded and looks identical everywhere.

**Note on the previous fign.org:** the old site (in `../old-fign-website/`) used `primary: #6C4AB6` (purple) + `accent: #FF2F92` (pink). That palette is *explicitly replaced* by the v3 editorial direction. "Similar to fign.org" here means the spirit (unique, brand-specific, intentional) — not the old palette.
